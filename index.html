About the catalog

RVMT was assembled from 5,150 globally distributed metatranscriptomes, expanding the known RNA-virosphere across all five established phyla and adding several proposed new ones.

Phyla

Five established RNA-virus phyla — Lenar-, Pisu-, Kitrino-, Duplorna- and Negarna-viricota — plus candidate phyla newly proposed from RVMT.

Hosts

RNA bacteriophages dominate microbial communities; eukaryotic viruses span Metazoa, Viridiplantae, Fungi, and microbial eukaryotes.

Provenance

Aggregated from JGI's IMG/M, Callanan et al. 2020, Wolf et al. 2020, Lauber et al. 2019, the MATOU expedition, and RefSeq/GenBank.

07 / Genome architecture

Three flavors of RNA.

Every RNA virus copies its genome with an RNA-dependent RNA polymerase, but the genome itself comes in three Baltimore classes — positive-sense single-strand, negative-sense single-strand, and double-strand. Each maps to a different evolutionary branch of the virosphere.

Class IV · (+)ssRNA

Positive-sense ssRNA

Genome is itself an mRNA — ribosomes can translate it directly upon entry. The largest and most diverse class.

08 / Biology

Five phyla, five life-styles.

Each phylum has a characteristic genome architecture, host repertoire, and set of signature proteins. Familiar pathogens you may know — and the metatranscriptomic dark matter that surrounds them.

Class IV · (+)ssRNA

Lenarviricota

119,074 viruses · the bacterial RNA virosphere
Hosts
Bacteria, fungi (mitochondrial), some plants
Genome
~3–5 kb, monopartite (+)ssRNA
Signature
Maturation protein, β-sheet coat protein, RdRp
Notable
Leviviridae phages MS2, Qβ; mitoviruses; narnaviruses
RVMT massively expanded this phylum — formerly only ~50 species, now tens of thousands of bacterial RNA phages discovered in metatranscriptomes worldwide.
Class IV · (+)ssRNA

Pisuviricota

118,672 viruses · the polyprotein lineage
Hosts
Animals, plants, fungi, protists
Genome
~7–30 kb, polyprotein expression strategy
Signature
Single jelly-roll capsid (CP_SJR), 3C-like protease, helicase, RdRp
Notable
Picornaviridae (poliovirus, hep A), Caliciviridae (norovirus), Coronaviridae (SARS-CoV-2)
Includes the Picornavirales super-order — 81,449 contigs in RVMT — and the Nidovirales, the longest known RNA genomes.
Class IV · (+)ssRNA

Kitrinoviricota

74,817 viruses · the alpha/flavi-like lineage
Hosts
Animals, plants, arthropod vectors
Genome
~9–13 kb, monopartite or multipartite
Signature
RdRp + helicase + methyltransferase cassette; capping machinery
Notable
Flaviviridae (dengue, Zika, hep C), Togaviridae (chikungunya), Tobamoviruses (TMV)
Home to nearly all arboviruses of public-health concern as well as a sprawling diversity of plant viruses.
Class III · dsRNA

Duplornaviricota

8,202 viruses · the encapsidated replication branch
Hosts
Animals, fungi, plants, protists, bacteria (Cystoviridae)
Genome
dsRNA, often segmented (Reoviridae has 9–12 segments)
Signature
RdRp held inside the capsid; T=1 / T=2 inner shells
Notable
Rotavirus, Reoviridae, Totiviridae yeast viruses, ϕ6 phage
dsRNA is a potent immune trigger — these viruses solved the problem by replicating their genomes inside an icosahedral protein cage, never exposing free dsRNA to the cell.
Class V · (−)ssRNA

Negarnaviricota

7,292 viruses · the antisense lineage
Hosts
Animals, plants, arthropods, fungi
Genome
(−)ssRNA, segmented or monopartite
Signature
L (large) RdRp protein, nucleocapsid, glycoprotein spikes
Notable
Influenza, Ebola, rabies, measles, hantaviruses, bunyaviruses
Unable to be translated directly — every virion must package its own polymerase to make sense-strand mRNA after entry.
Candidate · proposed

Candidate phyla

50,196 viruses · the dark matter
Lineages
p.0001/p.0002 sister to Kitrinoviricota; "Unclassified" RdRp clades
Genome
Mostly (+)ssRNA, novel architectures
Signature
RdRp homologs that fall outside all five established phyla
RVMT proposed five new candidate phyla — Taraviricota, Pomiviricota, Paraxenoviricota, Wamoviricota, Arctiviricota — extending the global RNA phylogeny.
09 / Protein clans

The most abundant ancient folds.

Profile clans group HMMs that share a common ancestral fold. The dominance of just a handful of clans across millions of viral ORFs reveals how deeply conserved the RNA-virus protein toolkit really is.

10 / Glossary

The viral protein toolkit.

A short field guide to the recurring HMM profiles in this catalog — what each protein does and why it matters.

RdRp
RNA-dependent RNA polymerase
The defining enzyme of every RNA virus — copies RNA from an RNA template. Its right-hand fold is one of the oldest enzymatic structures known and serves as the universal phylogenetic marker for the virosphere.
CP_SJR
Single jelly-roll capsid
An eight-stranded β-barrel that builds icosahedral viral shells. The most ancient and most reused capsid fold, found across Pisuviricota, Kitrinoviricota and beyond.
CP_Levi
Leviviridae coat protein
A unique β-sheet coat fold found only in (+)ssRNA bacteriophages. Self-assembles into T=3 icosahedral shells around a single genome copy.
Maturation_Levi
Phage maturation protein
A single-copy structural protein that lets RNA phages adsorb to the F-pilus of bacteria. Without it, the phage can package but cannot infect.
Helicase
SF1/SF2/SF3 helicase
An ATP-driven motor that unwinds duplex or structured RNA so the polymerase can advance. Common in Pisu- and Kitrino-viricota replication modules.
3CL_pro
3C-like protease
A chymotrypsin-fold protease that cleaves the long viral polyprotein into mature subunits — a major drug target (e.g., paxlovid against SARS-CoV-2).
L_protein
Large polymerase complex
In (−)ssRNA viruses, a multidomain enzyme combining the RdRp with capping, methyltransferase and polyadenylation activities — a one-stop transcription machine.
MTase
Methyltransferase
Caps the 5′ end of viral mRNA so it mimics a host transcript and escapes innate-immune sensors. Often fused to the RdRp in (+)ssRNA viruses.
Glycoprotein
Envelope spike
A membrane-anchored protein that binds host receptors and fuses the viral envelope with cellular membranes — the receptor-recognition handshake.
